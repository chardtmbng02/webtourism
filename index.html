About Pampanga

Learn More About Pampanga

Pampanga, located in Central Luzon, Philippines, has a rich history that spans pre-colonial times to the present day. Here are some key points in the history of Pampanga:

  • Pre-Colonial Era: Before the arrival of the Spanish, Pampanga was inhabited by the Aetas, an indigenous group. The Kapampangan people, the predominant ethnic group in the region, also had a thriving culture.
  • Colonization: The Spanish arrived in the Philippines in the 16th century. Pampanga became one of the first areas to be colonized. The Spanish influence is evident in the local culture, architecture, and the introduction of Christianity.
  • Mount Pinatubo Eruption: In 1991, Mount Pinatubo, located on the borders of Pampanga, erupted in one of the most significant volcanic eruptions of the 20th century. The eruption caused widespread destruction and had a lasting impact on the landscape and communities in the region.

What to do?

Pampanga offers a diverse range of activities for visitors, from exploring historical sites to indulging in its renowned culinary delights. Here are some things you can do in Pampanga:

Learn More

Culinary Tour

Pampanga is famous for its delicious cuisine. Explore local markets, eateries, and restaurants to savor traditional dishes such as sisig and tocino.

Historical Tour

Check out the historical churches in Pampanga, such as the St. Peter the Apostle Church in Apalit, and San Guillermo Parish Church in Bacolor.

Hot Air Balloon

Don't miss the annual Hot Air Balloon Festival in Clark. It's a spectacular event featuring hot air balloon flights, and other exciting activities.

Historical

Nayong Pilipino

The park features replicas of tourist spots, historical sites, ancestral homes and native villages from all over the Philippines.

Clark Museum

The Clark Museum showcases the history of Clark, the American occupation in the Philippines, and its significance in World War II

Museo Ning Angeles

The museum, which opened in 1999, gives a glimpse of Angeles City’s history through its memorabilia display.

Pamintuan Mansion

It is where the first Philippine President, Emilio Aguinaldo, stood to celebrate the first anniversary of Philippine independence from Spain.

San Fernando Train Station

This train station ruins served as a silent witness to the suffering of Filipino and American soldiers under the Japanese forces right after the fall of Bataan.

Holy Rosary Parish Church

Fondly called by locals as Pisambang Maragul (“Big Church”), the Holy Rosary Parish Church is a living church and a national historical site in Angeles, Pampanga.

4X4 Ride Mt. Pinatubo

Ride a 4x4 to the jump-off point where you will begin your trek. Enjoy the rugged landscape as you pass through rocky trails and idyllic streams.

Sandbox

Blow off the stress from the week and go on a weekend of thrilling activities at Sandbox in Porac, one of the best places to visit in Pampanga.

Sky Ranch

There’s always something about amusement parks that makes even the most jaded of adults feel like an innocent, carefree child again.

Aqua Planet

Head over to Aqua Planet in Clark Freeport Zone, Clark, Pampanga and enjoy a full day exploring its 30-plus water slides and other attractions.

Miyamit Falls

Wash away your stress by taking a dip at Miyamit Falls in Porac, Pampanga. Reaching this hidden gem at the heart of the province involves a long trek, but the view is worth the sweat.

Bird Watching

For sightseeing in Pampanga, visit the Candaba Swamp where you see white egrets, Philippine ducks, white-breasted herons, streaked reed-warbler, and other birds.
